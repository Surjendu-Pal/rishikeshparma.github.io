About Me

I’m Rishikesh Parma, a PhD researcher at the University of Luxembourg, working at the crossroads of optimization, uncertainty, and machine learning. I design smart, simple algorithms that help real-world supply chains make better decisions — not just in theory, but in practice. My work lives where math meets impact. I’ve presented at global conferences, built tools used by industry, and collaborated with brilliant minds across borders. Off the whiteboard, I’m into cricket, yoga, and long runs — because solving tough problems needs a clear head and a steady rhythm.

Research & Publications

My research focuses on capacity allocation in uncertain environments, combining optimization techniques with machine learning to tackle real-world supply chain challenges. I have developed deterministic auction models and contributed to several high-impact publications.

  • Mathematics of Operations Research (Under revision)
    “Simple and Effective Auctions” — explores enhanced allocation using support information.
  • Production and Operations Management (Working paper)
    “Balancing Contracts vs Spot Opportunities” — analyzes trade-offs in freight logistics.
  • Journal of the Operational Research Society (2022)
    Investigates outcome uncertainty in netball sports analytics.
  • European Journal of Operational Research (2019)
    Predicts scoring and outcomes in international rugby.

These contributions reflect a strong integration of academic rigor with industry relevance, consistently pushing the boundaries between theoretical research and practical application.

Teaching & Projects

I’ve contributed to academia as a co-lecturer and teaching assistant in several university-level courses:

  • Co-Lecturer: Linear Algebra (Bachelor’s, 2025), Supply Chain Management (Bachelor’s, 2025)
  • Teaching Assistant: Linear Algebra (2022–2023), Inventory Management (Master’s, 2024)
  • Supervisor: Master’s theses on freight demand forecasting (3 students)

In industry, I developed a decision-support tool for a maritime freight carrier that enables real-time contract allocation decisions by modeling demand across contract and spot markets. This tool improved revenue predictability and operational flexibility.

I also held roles as a Market Research Analyst in India and a Research Intern in the UK, gaining insights into data-driven market dynamics and academic research methods.
